# Gold Oil Risk Radar

Use this skill when a lesson or research note involves gold, crude oil, geopolitical shocks, inflation, rates, currencies, commodity ETFs, energy stocks, gold miners, or oil and gas funds.

## Gold Checklist

- Price: LBMA, CME COMEX, or clearly named market proxy.
- Rates: nominal yield, real yield, inflation expectations, and central bank policy.
- Currency: dollar strength and reserve-currency concerns.
- Demand: jewelry, bars and coins, ETF flows, central bank purchases, technology use.
- Positioning: futures positioning or ETF holdings when relevant.
- Risk: war, sanctions, financial stress, fiscal credibility, or liquidity stress.

## Oil Checklist

- Price: Brent, WTI, futures curve, and benchmark spread.
- Supply: OPEC+, U.S. shale, spare capacity, sanctions, outages, and shipping routes.
- Demand: transport, aviation, manufacturing, petrochemicals, China, U.S., Europe, and emerging markets.
- Inventory: EIA weekly petroleum report, OECD inventories, floating storage when available.
- Refining: crack spreads, refinery utilization, product inventories.
- Transmission: oil price to inflation, rates, consumer spending, margins, equities, bonds, currencies, and funds.

## Required Sources

- Gold: World Gold Council, LBMA, CME COMEX, ETF issuer pages, central bank or IMF reserve data.
- Oil: EIA, IEA, OPEC, CME NYMEX, ICE Brent, government statements, company filings.
- Geopolitics: official statements first; use authoritative media only with source dates and uncertainty labels.

## Output Rule

Always state the data cutoff and classify each claim as confirmed fact, inference, or watch item.
