# Political Economy Bridge

Use this skill when a lesson needs to connect markets with politics, institutions, policy, and social rules.

## Core Framing

- Economics studies choices under scarcity.
- Politics and institutions shape the rules under which choices are made.
- Markets react not only to resources and profits, but also to policy, regulation, credibility, enforcement, war, sanctions, and social expectations.

## Neutral Analysis Pattern

When political or institutional topics appear, separate:

1. Confirmed institutional fact: law, policy, regulation, official data, treaty, central bank action, budget, or sanction.
2. Incentive structure: which actors gain, lose, pay, receive, borrow, lend, produce, consume, regulate, or enforce.
3. Market transmission: effect on profits, costs, inflation, interest rates, currency, risk appetite, commodities, and funds.
4. Uncertainty: what is not yet confirmed, what could change, and which indicators to watch.

## Safety Rules

- Do not reduce political economy to a single motive or one-party explanation.
- Do not present unverified claims as facts.
- Prefer official documents, central banks, finance ministries, regulators, exchanges, international organizations, and primary reports.
- When using media or commentary, label it as secondary analysis and check the underlying source.
