# Financial Product Map

Use this skill when a lesson needs to turn scattered financial products into a beginner-friendly map.

## Teaching Order

1. Start with the relationship behind the product, not the product name.
2. Classify basic products first:
   - Ownership relationship: stocks, ownership-like commodities, some real assets with financial attributes.
   - Debt-credit relationship: government bonds, local government bonds, corporate bonds, deposits.
   - Trust or entrusted-management relationship: funds, some trusts, collective investment products.
   - Currency exchange relationship: foreign exchange and exchange rates.
3. Introduce derivatives only after the learner understands basic products:
   - Forwards, futures, options, swaps.
   - Explain underlying asset, future settlement, contract, leverage, margin, hedging, speculation, and zero-sum intuition.
4. Explain why a fund is not automatically a derivative even if its value changes with stocks, bonds, gold, or oil.

## Required Beginner Explanations

- For every English term or acronym, provide Chinese translation, plain-language explanation, and why it matters.
- For every product, answer:
  - What relationship does it represent?
  - Who pays whom?
  - Where might the return come from?
  - What can go wrong?
  - Is it mainly ownership, lending, entrusted management, currency exchange, or a future contract?

## Source Discipline

- Treat user-provided videos or subtitles as teaching inspiration, not factual authority.
- Verify product rules, regulation, exchange mechanics, and policy details from official sources before presenting them as facts.
